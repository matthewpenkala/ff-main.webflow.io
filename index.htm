Hey, I'm Matt, VFX director and maker

Hello, my name is Matthew Penkala. For as long as I can remember, I was captivated by the entire spectacle of film production and visual effects and found myself fascinated by all of the graphics, lighting, and sound. The idea of telling an impactful story through the "lens" of a short film or cinematic documentary brought out a passion I hadn't known existed. Whatever this bizarre feeling was that I experienced, I knew one thing for sure: I wanted to be a part of creating it. I am a highly motivated and development-oriented person who enjoys problem-solving with a hands-on attitude. Attention to detail and specifics has always been a crucial part of any role that I have completed. Ensuring the efficient and effective delivery of quality products and services is a matter of personal and professional pride.

In my earlier years and after briefly shadowing a few production veterans at Bayside Church in Northern California (the fastest-growing church nationwide in 2019), I was assigned to a broadcast camera and quickly became enthralled with live video. Soon my ambitions led me to the central control room. The first time I stepped inside, I thought I was on the flight deck of a modern jetliner:  AV components, control surfaces, OLED monitors, computers, and fiber optic cables filled the space. I had no idea how any of the equipment and software worked but felt an overwhelming desire to master it. After countless hours over the next several months, I began directing the entire video production crew in Bayside's control studio for events with thousands of people both on-campus and through online streaming. The senior video director soon challenged me to yet another seemingly impossible task:  create motion graphics for major upcoming events displayed on massive LED walls. To accomplish this, I pulled many all-nighters, teaching myself Adobe After Effects. My final creation far surpassed the staff's expectations and amazed other departments who noticed my creativity and determination.

While at Bayside, I thrived in a professional environment with high standards, time pressures, supportive staff, and a high level of personal accountability that encouraged everyone to perform at their best. Countless hours of preparation and collaboration amongst my group fostered comradery and the constant quest for individual improvement. I learned that team cohesiveness is vital to overcoming the unpredictable setbacks and challenges inherent in live production. The quality of the team environment is as essential as the professionalism of the final product.

Matthew T. Penkala

Motion GFX & VFX Freelancer
